About Quick Groups

A self-service CRUD interface for grouping ActiveDirectory names.

Use cases

  • Finding the right people to include on a new e-mail thread
    • This use is intended for involving relevant personnel in topical discussions by launching an email client with pre-populated addresses. For important discussions whose content should be a matter of public record within an organization (for example, making long-term policy decisions), you should use a mailing list service like Mailman.
  • Skills inventory: broadcasting available skills in an organization
  • Dynamic groups that change faster than it would take the AD admin(s) to update them

Design Goals / Motivation

  • Distributed-maintenance
  • Avoids AD administrator turnaround time
  • Lighter-weight and less "official" than actual AD groups
  • "Lighter-weight" than GNU Mailman mailing lists
  • Group creation does not require AD administrator approval
  • Could be used for "drafting" true AD groups
    • Add a feature to "submit draft" to AD administrator to apply to the authoritative AD?
  • Query-able without requiring AD credentials

Design choices

I wanted to be able to infer new groups from existing groups. There are two opposing approaches for this:

  • Make specific groups the basic unit, infer larger groups through union operations
  • Make general categories (i.e. "tags") the basic unit, infer more specific groups through intersection operations
I've chosen the former approach.

Limits of Scope

This project shall not become standalone "announcement list" or "discussion list" software, with the ability to send and store messages. Rather, it shall only be a method of obtaining lists of people for which to communicate by other means.
